Notable publications

  • 2021
    KH Lemke | Structure and solvation dynamics of the hydroxide ion in ice-like water clusters, a CCSD(T) and car–parrinello molecular dynamics study 📃 | Phys.Chem.Chem.Phys., 23, 18990.
    • This CPMD study examines hydroxide-water clusters, reporting structures and temperature-dependent behavior, and hopw these impact OH- ion solvation in nanodroplets and aqueous interfaces.
  • 2018
    KH Lemke, TM Seward | Molecular clusters and solvation in volcanic and hydrothermal vapors 📃 | Rev.Min.Geochem., 84, 57.
    • This paper represents the culmination of a long collaboration with Terry Seward at the ETH Zürich, in which we examined steam and water vapor chemistry in the Earth's crust.
  • 2017
    KH Lemke | Structure and binding energy of the H2S dimer at the CCSD(T) complete basis set limit 📃 | J.Chem.Phys, 146, 234301.
    • This study employs CCSD(T)/CBS theory to explore binding in the H2S dimer, showcasing the importance of HS...H hydrogen-bonds in fields such as protein chemistry, physical chemistry, and metal transport in the Earth's crust.
  • 2013
    KH Lemke, TM Seward | Thermodynamic properties of carbon dioxide clusters by M06-2X and dispersion-corrected B2PLYP-D theory 📃 | J.Chem.Phys, 146, 234301.
    • This is a DFT study (with Terry Seward), in which we analyze (CO2)n clusters, finding that icosahedral (CO2)13 plays a key role for understanding carbon dioxide ice formation in Mars's atmosphere.

Academic Interests

  • Research: FT ICR ion mass spectrometry, IRMPD spectroscopy of metal ion complexes and clusters, metals in ore fluids and vapors, static (G16,C4) and dynamic (CPMD/CP2K ) ab initio calculations of cluster structures and energies.
  • Undergraduate Teaching: 1. EASC2401 "Fluid/Solid Interactions in Earth Processes" Physical and chemical principles governing Earth processes, focusing on fluid-solid interactions, and covering topics such as thermo-dynamics, states of matter, mineral-solution interfaces, energy exchange, kinetics, and fluid flow. 2. CCGL9033 "Weapons of Mass Destruction - Science, Proliferation and Terrorism" This course examines the development, deterrence, and risks of weapons of mass destruction (WMD), addressing their strategic, political, and ethical aspects, non-proliferation efforts, and the potential consequences of terror networks acquiring WMD materials, while exploring the feasibility and desirability of disarmament.
  • Gggggggggraduate Teaching: 1. EASC6010 "Nanogeoscience" course covers nanoscience and nanotechnology concepts, the role of nanomaterials in Earth's history and life's origins, nanophase diagrams, size-dependent properties, analytical techniques, applications in Earth sciences, and emerging trends in the field.
